What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is the use of digital technologies like social media, text messages, or websites to harass, threaten, or embarrass someone. It can happen to anyone and can be deeply damaging.

How to Recognize It

  • Sending threatening or mean messages
  • Spreading lies or rumors online
  • Sharing private information or photos without consent
  • Excluding someone from online groups

What You Can Do

If you're being cyberbullied or see it happening:

  • Don't respond or retaliate
  • Block the person and report the behavior
  • Talk to someone you trust
  • Keep evidence (screenshots, messages)
